## What Wave 2 got right (worth pinning)

The 2 new SEV1s are real regressions and warrant fixing before public launch, but they should not obscure that Wave 2 also did well on substance:

- **Issue 84's audit was honest about its own ceiling.** It surfaced ~$0.027/clip in Haiku-4.5 cost savings + 10-15% input-token waste on two unused cache markers + the SDK-bump observability gap, but shipped only the 1-LOC web_search bump because the other findings genuinely needed their own assess cycles. That discipline is what kept the Wave-2 commit footprint small enough to reason about line-by-line.
- **Issue 92's deterministic-stream-key design** (`video_id`/`clip_id` over Celery task IDs) is correct — it dodges chain-id propagation entirely and gives the frontend an identifier it already has. The two new SEV1s are not in that design; they're in the streaming-extension wiring (improvement brief) and the router ordering. The primitive itself is sound.
- **Test surface grew correctly:** +11 tests, all on the new behavior. `tests/test_progress_emit_wiring.py` pins the emit sequence, stream-key choice, and router stamping per surface — a future refactor that drops an emit will fail CI loudly.
- **Zero new BLOCKERs, zero cross-tenant leak surface, zero SEV1 introduced by Issue 84** (the regression-introducing surface is entirely on Issue 92's streaming wrapper extension).
